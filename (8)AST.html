Alternating Harmonic Series

Harmonic Series

Explanation:

The harmonic series is defined as:

\( H_n = \sum_{k=1}^n \frac{1}{k} \)

This series diverges as \( n \to \infty \), meaning it grows without bound.

The alternating harmonic series is defined as:

\( A_n = \sum_{k=1}^n \frac{(-1)^{k+1}}{k} \)

This series converges to \( \ln(2) \) as \( n \to \infty \), because the terms decrease in magnitude and alternate in sign.

Thus we define the alternating series test (AST):
An alternating series converges if the terms are growing smaller and the infinitiest term is 0
