Taylor Series

\( e^x \approx c_0 + c_1 x + c_2 x^2 \). Let \( g(x) = c_0 + c_1 x + c_2 x^2\).

Recall that a degree two approximation is just a degree two polynomial, which is why our approximation is
initially just the general form of a degree two polynomial.

  • \( g(0) = e^0 \)
  • \( f'(0) = \left. \frac{d}{dx} e^x \right|_{x=0} \)
  • \( f''(0) = \left. \frac{d^2}{dx^2} e^x \right|_{x=0} \)
\[ g(0) = e^0 \Rightarrow c_0 = 1 \] \[ f'(x) = c_1 \] \[ \frac{d}{dx} e^x = e^x \] \[ f'(0) = \left. \frac{d}{dx} e^x \right|_{x=0} = e^0 = 1 \Rightarrow c_1 = 1 \] \[ g(x) = 1+x \] \[ f''(0) = \left. \frac{d}{dx} e^x \right|_{x=0} = e^0 = 1 \Rightarrow c_1 = 1 \] \[ g(x) = 1+x \]
